What Is A Pay Stub?

Simply put, a pay stub is a paper we keep after cashing our payroll checks. Not only does a pay stub serve as proof of income, it also helps you keep track of salary information, taxes paid, overtime pay and more.

A Paystub is a document usually given by an employer that outlines and details your pay (net pay- that is, the amount you take home) This paystub document lists and itemizes your salary/ wages earned for a specific set period. It also lists your taxes and other deductions taken out of your paycheck.

A paystub document has many names and you can even use them interchangeably. A pay stub, pay slip, check stub, earnings statement or a paycheck stub all refer to the same document. An employee or an individual can create pay stubs online using the check stub maker or more commonly known as the paystub generator.

Depending on the state you reside in, a pay stub adheres to a certain set of rules. Some states mandate that a paystub must be printed and given to employees in hardcopy. Other states are not as stringent with their rules and allow employees to have an option of hard or soft copies of their paystub.
