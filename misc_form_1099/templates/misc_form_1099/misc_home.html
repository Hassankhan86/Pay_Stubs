What Are The Exceptions When Reporting A 1099 Income?

There are a few exceptions when reporting your income on a 1099 or 1099 online. Some payments need not be reported through a 1099, although they could be taxable. In that case, you would need to fill in other documents such as form W2 perhaps or a form 1098 even. Let’s explore some of these exceptions.

  • Payments Made For Certain Commodities Such As A Telephone, Cargo, Storage, Or Any Other Such Related Items.
  • Payments Made Out To Real Estate Agents Or Property Managers. However, The Real Estate Broker Should Report The Rent Paid Over To The Property Owner.
  • Payments Made To Employees While They Are On Active Duty In The Armed Forces Or Similar Uniformed Services.
  • Payments Made To Employees On Business Travel.
  • Payments Or Any Compensation Made For The Wrongful Incarceration For Any Criminal Offense.

These are just some of the exceptions listed to give you an idea. You can find the full list on the IRS Website so that you can stay informed and up to date.

How Do I File A 1099 Online?

If you have been given a 1099 physical form you can mail in your tax return. If however, you have created or been given a 1099 online, and you have filled it out, here’s how to file 1099 online easily.

First, you need to make sure that you have several copies of your 1099 on hand so that you can send copies to the following concerned parties by or before January 31st at the very latest.

  • The IRS
  • The State Tax Department
  • The Contractor
  • For Your Own Records

Below are some of the ways you can file 1099, however that depends on how you have registered or incorporated your business.

If you, as an independent contractor, have registered yourself as a company with a single member as in, an LLC/Sole Proprietor, then your income is reported on a schedule C form. You need to remember to add your 1099 income to all your other revenue sources and mention it on line 12 of your tax return.

If in the instance that you have registered as a multi-member LLC or corporation, then your filing methods will be a bit different. Rather than filling your 1099 under your personal income tax, you would need to include it on your business tax return in the total income section.

As a business employing a contractor or freelancer, you will need to submit a 1096 form to the IRS to summarize all of your 1099 misc forms that you have issued over a set period of time.

What Are The 1099 Penalties I Should Be Aware Of?

As always, there are some IRS penalties that you need to be aware of and try your very best to avoid.

  • Late 1099 Form Filing.
  • Filing A 1099 Form That Is Not Machine-Readable.
  • Failure To Include All The Necessary Information.
  • Failure To File Electronically When You Were Requested To.
  • Filing With Incorrect Or Missing TIN

Filing form 1099 within 30 days or less after its due date gives you a penalty of $50 per form you send in. This can increase up to $100 per form if it exceeds 30 days.
